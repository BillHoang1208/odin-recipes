Vietnamese Beef Pho

Description

Beef Pho, a quintessential Vietnamese comfort food, is a fragrant and soul-warming noodle soup that captivates with its depth of flavor and aromatic spices. At its heart lies a rich and clear broth, brewed meticulously over hours to extract the essence of beef bones, infused with spices like star anise, cinnamon, cloves, and cardamom, resulting in a broth that is both robust and delicately perfumed. Thinly sliced tender beef, typically raw or lightly cooked, is added to the piping hot broth just before serving, allowing it to cook gently in the warmth of the soup. The soup is generously ladled over soft, translucent rice noodles, which soak up the flavors of the broth while retaining their satisfying chewiness. The bowl is then garnished with an assortment of fresh herbs like Thai basil, cilantro, and culantro, along with crisp bean sprouts, sliced chili peppers, and lime wedges, allowing diners to customize each spoonful to their liking. With its complex interplay of flavors and textures, Beef Pho offers a comforting and invigorating culinary experience that transcends its humble origins, earning it a cherished place in Vietnamese cuisine and the hearts of food lovers around the world.

Ingredients

  • Beef bones (such as oxtail, marrow bones, or beef knuckle)
  • Water
  • Yellow onion, peeled and halved
  • Ginger, sliced
  • Star anise
  • Cinnamon stick
  • Cloves
  • Cardamom pods
  • Salt
  • Sugar
  • Fish sauce
  • Rice noodles (banh pho)
  • Beef (such as sirloin or flank), thinly sliced
  • Fresh herbs (such as Thai basil, cilantro, and culantro)
  • Bean sprouts
  • Chili peppers, sliced (optional)
  • Lime wedges

Steps

  1. Place beef bones in a large stockpot and cover with water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce the heat to low and simmer gently for at least 1 hour (ideally 4-6 hours), skimming any foam that rises to the surface.
  2. Add halved onion, sliced ginger, star anise, cinnamon stick, cloves, and cardamom pods to the pot. Continue to simmer, uncovered, for another 1-2 hours, allowing the flavors to meld and the broth to develop depth.
  3. Season the broth with salt, sugar, and fish sauce to taste, adjusting the seasoning as needed to achieve a balance of savory, sweet, and salty flavors.
  4. Strain the broth through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth to remove the solids, leaving behind a clear and flavorful broth.
  5. Meanwhile, cook the rice noodles according to package instructions until they are al dente. Drain and rinse the noodles under cold water to stop the cooking process and prevent them from sticking together.
  6. Divide the cooked noodles among serving bowls and top with thinly sliced raw beef.
  7. Bring the strained broth back to a simmer and ladle it over the noodles and beef, ensuring that the beef is fully submerged in the hot broth.
  8. Serve the beef pho piping hot, garnished with fresh herbs like Thai basil, cilantro, and culantro, along with bean sprouts, sliced chili peppers (if using), and lime wedges on the side.
  9. Allow diners to customize their bowls with additional herbs, sprouts, chili peppers, and lime juice according to their taste preferences. Enjoy the comforting and aromatic flavors of homemade beef pho!
