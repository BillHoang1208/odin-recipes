Pad Thai

Description

Pad Thai is a beloved and iconic Thai dish renowned for its harmonious blend of flavors and textures. It typically consists of stir-fried rice noodles, which are cooked to perfection, tender yet with a delightful chewiness. The dish is often enriched with a choice of protein, such as succulent shrimp, tender chicken, or tofu, lending a satisfying heartiness to each bite. Complementing the noodles and protein are a colorful array of fresh vegetables, including crisp bean sprouts and vibrant green onions, adding a refreshing crunch and a burst of freshness. What truly sets Pad Thai apart, however, is its signature sauceâ€”a harmonious fusion of tangy tamarind, savory fish sauce, subtle sweetness from sugar, and a hint of heat from chili flakes, perfectly balancing sweet, sour, salty, and spicy notes. Garnished with a generous sprinkle of chopped roasted peanuts and a squeeze of zesty lime, Pad Thai tantalizes the palate with its complex yet harmonious flavor profile, making it a favorite among Thai cuisine enthusiasts worldwide.

Ingredients

  • Rice noodles
  • Vegetable oil
  • Garlic, minced
  • Shrimp, chicken, or tofu (optional protein)
  • Eggs
  • Bean sprouts
  • Green onions, chopped
  • Roasted peanuts, chopped
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (optional)
  • Lime wedges
  • Pad Thai sauce (usually made with tamarind paste, fish sauce, soy sauce, sugar, and sometimes chili flakes)

Steps

  1. Soak rice noodles in warm water for about 30 minutes until they become pliable but still firm. Drain and set aside.
  2. Prepare Pad Thai sauce by combining tamarind paste, fish sauce, soy sauce, sugar, and chili flakes in a bowl. Adjust the ingredients to achieve the desired balance of sweet, sour, salty, and spicy flavors.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add minced garlic and cook until fragrant.
  4. If using protein such as shrimp, chicken, or tofu, add it to the skillet and cook until it's almost fully cooked through.
  5. Push the protein to one side of the skillet and crack eggs into the empty space. Scramble the eggs until they are cooked through, then mix them with the protein.
  6. Add soaked rice noodles to the skillet and stir-fry until they are tender and coated with the other ingredients.
  7. Pour the Pad Thai sauce over the noodles and toss everything together until the noodles are evenly coated.
  8. Add bean sprouts and chopped green onions to the skillet and toss for another minute until they are heated through.
  9. Transfer the Pad Thai to serving plates and garnish with chopped roasted peanuts, fresh cilantro (if using), and lime wedges.
  10. Serve hot and enjoy your delicious homemade Pad Thai!
